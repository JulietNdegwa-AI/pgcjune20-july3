Analysis

1. First response time (FRT)
AI agent: Held steady at 6s, maintaining near-instant response capability with no change.
Human agent: Dropped by 11s (27s → 38s), a notable slowdown despite the overall drop in ticket volume.

2. Median close time
AI agent: Essentially flat, dropping by just 1s (5m 9s → 5m 10s), showing continued consistency in resolution speed.
Human agent: Improved by 2m 12s (10m 1s → 7m 49s), likely benefiting from the 12% drop in total ticket volume, easing pressure on human agents this period.

3. Customer satisfaction (CSAT)
AI agent: Dropped sharply from 66.7% to 20% (-46.7%). Sample size grew slightly (3 → 5 CSATs) but remains very small; at this volume, one or two dissatisfied responses can swing the score dramatically, so this decline should be treated as a signal to watch rather than a confirmed trend.
Human agent: Declined modestly from 90.5% to 87.5% (-3%) on a shrinking but still reasonably reliable sample (21 → 14 CSATs), suggesting a mild softening in service quality rather than a major shift.

4. Automation & volume
Total ticket volume: Decreased from 234 to 206 tickets (-28, a ~12% drop).
AI containment: Fell from 33% to 29% (-4%). In absolute terms, AI closed fewer tickets (76 → 60), and the proportion of the overall workload absorbed by AI also shrank due to the 24+ hours shutdown.
